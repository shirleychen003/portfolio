    1. Overview

    Have you ever had issues managing a subscription, forgotten about a free trial that you signed for, or gone through a tedious process attempting to cancel a service? Subscription services are supposed to be considered tools to facilitate everyday tasks, so why can so many problems arise within the subscriptions framework? Subscriptions Super Simplified (3S) was created with the objective to solve the aforementioned issues along with facilitating any processes while interacting with a subscription service. By using this app, users are able to oversee their subscriptions with ease and eliminate any unnecessary stresses.

    This app was created as a group project for Professor Olivier St. Cyr’s class INF352: Information Design Studio II: How to Design at the University of Toronto - St. George.

    2. Research

    To deepen our understanding of the issues we were trying to address, my group and I conducted thorough research using both secondary and primary research methods.

    Prior to conducting the primary research, we explored secondary sources such as news articles, current market statistics and reports, and scientific working papers to get an overview of the subject matter. The secondary research topics consisted of services and impact on subscriptions, existing public opinions and solutions, and current business models. Once we had a sufficient amount of foundational knowledge, we began to organize and send out primary research methods, which consisted of a survey through Google forms and individual in person interviews.

    By the end of the research process, we had collected 77 anonymous responses in the Google forms, and interviewed answers from 16 different individuals. The Google forms link had been posted on our Instagram stories, so anyone who had access to the link within the 24 hours before it expired was able to record their response. Our target demographic was subscription users, thus for the intersiews, we had two main requirements for the interviewees: they must have at least one subscription and pay for it, and they must have cancelled or tried to cancel at least one service in the past year. These interviews were conducted in person or over the phone. From our research, we were able to compile various struggles users had with their subscriptions and then pinpoint the similarities.

    75% of the Google forms participants had at least forgotten to cancel their subscription once and 73% relied on their memory to track their subscriptions.

    Using Miro, we created an affinity map where organized all of our interview data into sections to identify key themes.

    Along with the issue of forgetting to cancel their subscriptions after a free trial due to lack of tracking methods, the interviewees expressed that there is often a complicated and frustrating cancellation process involved with subscription services. After all our research was gathered and examined, we were ready to move onto the ideation process of the project.

    3. Ideation

    An essential part in our project was the ideation process, in which my group and I were able to practice collaboration and exercise our creative thinking.

    Part 1 of the ideation process included producing a persona, an empathy map, As-Is scenario, and need statements. Firstly, we generated a persona using data from our research and designed a potential scenario that would benefit from using our app. Our persona was Selena the Subscriber, a busy university student with lots of subscriptions.

    To gain a deeper understanding of Selena’s thoughts, motivations, and feelings, we curated an empathy map. Actual quotes and expressed feelings were derived from our research data. Using the empathy map, we created an As-Is scenario to walk through a potential real world scenario that Selena would experience when facing an issue with a subscription. The mapped out As-Is scenario clearly communicated the main pain points that Selena had.
